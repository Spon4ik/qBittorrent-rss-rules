# Document Quality Checklist

## Objective Fit

- Confirm the document goal and target reader are explicit.
- Confirm the artifact type matches the requested outcome.
- Confirm non-goals or out-of-scope boundaries are stated when relevant.

## Decision Completeness

- Confirm major choices include rationale and tradeoffs.
- Confirm owners, dependencies, and due dates are specified for follow-up actions.
- Confirm assumptions and open questions are separated from confirmed facts.

## Technical Grounding

- Confirm claims are traceable to code, test results, logs, metrics, or stakeholder input.
- Confirm file paths, commands, and interfaces are concrete where implementation is discussed.
- Confirm acceptance criteria are testable and unambiguous.

## Resumability

- Confirm the current state is clear: complete, in progress, and next.
- Confirm next actions are immediately executable without additional discovery.
- Confirm important context is captured in documents, not only in chat history.

## Cross-Document Consistency

- Confirm roadmap, phase plan, and current status do not contradict each other.
- Confirm terminology is consistent across project, design, and QA artifacts.
- Confirm deferred work and risks are logged in exactly one source of truth each.

## Publication Gate

- Confirm time-sensitive items use exact dates (`YYYY-MM-DD`).
- Confirm language is concise and actionable.
- Confirm no unresolved TODO markers remain.
